# Choose Your Own Adventure — Startup Founding

You are creating an interactive "choose your own adventure" visual narrative about founding an AI startup in 2026. The viewer makes decisions at key moments that change the narrative direction. Present choices visually, then show the consequences unfolding as cinematic scenes.

## First Step

Discover the full component catalog before creating any scenes. Study what is available — especially Animate, Stagger, Presence, Card, Overlay, Gradient, Chart, and the broadcast components. You have a rich palette. Use it.

## Quality Standards

- **This is a visual film, not a slide deck.** Each scene should feel like a frame from a movie — composed, lit, atmospheric. Not centered text on a gradient. Not a grid of cards.
- **Continuity between scenes.** The story flows. Each scene transitions into the next with intentional pacing. Use Animate presets (slide-in-left, fade-in, scale-up) so scenes arrive with visual intention. Use Stagger to reveal choice cards one by one for dramatic effect.
- **Color signals mood.** Warm palettes for optimistic moments. Cool, dark tones for challenges. High contrast and dramatic compositions for pivotal decisions. The palette shifts with the narrative — this is how you create emotional continuity.
- **The presentation of choices IS the climax.** Pause. Reveal options with Stagger. Let the weight of the decision land. Then commit to a path and show what unfolds.
- **No generic AI aesthetic.** No centered-text-with-emoji. No uniform card grids. Each decision point deserves its own visual treatment.

## The Story

You are founding an AI startup in 2026. The AI gold rush is in full swing — venture capital is abundant but increasingly selective, competition is fierce, and the technology landscape shifts monthly. You have $50,000 in savings, 8 years of engineering experience with an AI/ML specialty, and a burning desire to build something that matters.

## Decision Points

Each decision leads to different scenes with different visual treatments:

1. **What problem to solve?** Three market opportunities, each with different risk/reward profiles. Present as dramatic, full-bleed visual cards — not a bullet list.
2. **Bootstrap or raise VC?** The classic founder dilemma. Show the two paths diverging visually — use Split to contrast timelines, constraints, tradeoffs.
3. **Who to hire first?** Your first hire shapes your company's DNA. Engineer, designer, or business person? Each option gets its own visual identity.
4. **Competitor launches a similar product.** Fight, differentiate, or pivot? A crisis — shift the color palette to tension. Use Banner or Overlay for urgency.
5. **Early data is mixed.** Double down or pivot? Use Chart or Sparkline to show the data that drives the decision. Let the numbers tell the story.

## Visual Approach

Build this as a VISUAL NARRATIVE with cinematic pacing. Each act has:
- **Establishing shot** — set the mood, introduce the context with atmospheric composition
- **Rising tension** — build toward the decision with Stagger-animated information reveals
- **The choice** — full dramatic presentation of options, each visually distinct
- **Consequences** — a new scene showing the path chosen, with visual continuity to what came before

Use hard cuts between acts and incremental updates for reveals within acts. The rhythm of cuts and builds IS the storytelling.
