# Onion News Network — Satirical Broadcast

You are producing an Onion News Network broadcast — a complete satirical news show that unfolds segment by segment like a real TV program. This is not a list of funny headlines. It is a produced broadcast with an opening, distinct segments that cut between each other, a dramatic BREAKING interruption, and a closing.

Deadpan delivery is everything — present absurd things as completely normal. The production should look SERIOUS and PROFESSIONAL. ALL the comedy comes from the content. A polished graphic reading "Jim Anchower, Area Lifestyle Correspondent" is funnier than a silly-looking one.

## First Step

Discover the full component catalog before producing anything. Study what is available — especially broadcast components (LowerThird, Ticker, Banner, Badge), animation components (Animate, Stagger, Presence), data components (Chart, Stat, Table), and layout tools (Split, Overlay, Grid). A professional news broadcast uses all of these. Your production quality should be indistinguishable from a real newscast — the comedy is purely in what the serious graphics SAY.

## Quality Standards

- **Broadcast television production quality.** This should look like CNN, BBC, or MSNBC — polished, professional, dead serious in presentation. The comedy comes entirely from the absurd content delivered through serious-looking production. A well-designed LowerThird reading "Area Man Discovers His Entire Personality Was Just A Phase" is peak Onion.
- **Continuity between segments.** The show flows like a real broadcast. Each segment cuts to the next with intentional pacing. Use Animate for cinematic transitions between segments. Let scenes breathe between segments — a beat of the title card, a ticker pause, a hold on a graphic.
- **Rich broadcast component usage.** Use LowerThird for EVERY correspondent attribution — this is non-negotiable for broadcast quality. Use Ticker throughout for running one-liner headlines. Use Banner for BREAKING NEWS alerts. Use Badge for location slugs and segment labels. Use Split for correspondent reports. Use Chart and Stat for poll results and data segments. Use Presence to animate breaking alerts in and out.
- **No generic AI aesthetic.** No centered text on gradient. No card grids. This looks like a REAL newscast. Professional typography. Broadcast layout conventions. The Onion's genius is that it looks exactly like real news — the absurdity is purely content.
- **Pacing is comedy.** Let the show breathe. A beat after a headline lands. A pause before cutting to the next segment. The rhythm of cuts IS the comedic timing. Do not rush.

## The Broadcast

Invent your own satirical headlines and produce your broadcast:

**Scene 1: Show Open** — Use Animate with fade-in for the title card: "ONION NEWS NETWORK" with a datestamp and the day's top satirical headline as a tease. Professional. Clean. Deadpan. Hold for impact.

**Scene 2: Lead Story** — Cut to the main story. Headline displayed prominently. Body text for the story. LowerThird for correspondent attribution ("Janet Hawkins, National Affairs Correspondent"). Badge for section label. The content is satirical — deadpan, absurd premises presented as completely normal. Use Stagger to build up the story elements.

**Scene 3: Area Man Segment** — Cut to a human interest piece. "Area Man [Does Something Absurd]." LowerThird with correspondent name. Badge for location slug. Different visual treatment from the lead — this is a lighter segment. Use Animate for the transition.

**Scene 4: BREAKING NEWS** — Interrupt the broadcast. Use Banner with severity "error" for maximum urgency. Use Presence to animate the alert in dramatically. Change the visual tone — red, urgent, loud visually. Present a breaking satirical story. Then use Presence to animate the alert out and settle back to normal broadcasting.

**Scene 5: Poll Results** — Cut to a data segment. "New Poll: 67% of Americans Would Rather Not." Use Chart (bar or pie) for the poll data. Use Stat for key percentages. Deadpan analysis presented through serious-looking data visualization. Use Stagger to reveal poll results one by one.

**Scene 6: Correspondent Report** — A field report from an absurdly-titled correspondent. Use Split for a two-panel composition. LowerThird for the absurd correspondent title. Badge for location. Different visual tone from the studio segments.

**Scene 7: Sign-Off** — Closing card with Animate entrance. A final one-liner. "This has been ONN." Use Stagger for credits. Professional, clean, minimal.

Throughout the entire broadcast, maintain Ticker with scrolling one-liner satirical headlines. Use hard cuts between segments and incremental updates for within-segment builds. Satire should be sharp, absurd, and genuinely funny. Deadpan. Satirize systems and the human condition, never individuals.
